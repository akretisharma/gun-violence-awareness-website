What is gun violence?

Gun violence refers to any form of firearm violence that threatens our right to live. It may include homicides, violent crimes, suicides, or unintentional deaths. Throughout 2019 and 2020, there was a 28% increase in gun-related homicides. The following year, in 2021, gun homicides increased another 7%. As well as affecting us economically, gun violence can also affect us mentally. As a result of firearm violence, there are many broken communities, families, and individuals. More than 85,000 Americans are injured by guns every year, and more than 38,000 people are killed by them.

Gun violence and Women

Gun violence is especially lethal in the hands of a partner. In 2017, more than 85,000 women were killed, intentionally. 58% of them were killed by partners or family members. This means globally, 137 women are killed by family members each day.

What can we do to help?

1. Establish gun violence prevention laws

2. Reduce gun access being given to children and teens

3. Recognizing gun violence as a public health problem

4. Giving money to help with community tramua and mental health

5. Restricting gun possession for those with a history of violence
